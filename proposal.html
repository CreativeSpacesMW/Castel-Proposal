THE BLUEPRINT

PROJECT PROPOSAL: THE CASTEL-WRLD DIGITAL ECOSYSTEM

Prepared for: Castel Malawi Ltd.

Objective: Transitioning from Static Information to Digital Operations & Market Dominance

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

In a rapidly evolving digital landscape, a brand's website must do more than simply exist; it must perform. The current Castel Malawi web presence serves as an informative brochure, but it lacks the interactive capabilities required to manage modern supply chains or engage the 21st-century consumer.

Castel-Wrld is a bespoke, full-stack digital hub designed to bridge the gap between the brewery, the distributor, and the end consumer. By integrating B2B order management, asset tracking (crates), and direct-to-retailer consumer engagement, we are proposing a tool that increases operational speed while providing Castel with unprecedented market data.

2. THE EVOLUTION: KEY DIFFERENCES

FEATURE CURRENT WEBSITE PROPOSED CASTEL-WRLD
User Experience Desktop-centric; high data usage Mobile-First; optimized for Malawi's network speeds
B2B Capabilities None; manual/offline ordering Tiered Ordering (Wholesale/Retail) with automated forms
Asset Management Tracking done via manual audits Digital Crate Tracking framework for distributors
Retail Support Simple directory Interactive "Bar of the Week" & GPS-enabled "Find My Drink" map
Revenue Stream Passive brand awareness Active lead generation and commission-based order facilitation
Content Strategy Static corporate info Dynamic "Community Stories" and gamified loyalty sections

3. PROJECT TIMELINE & IMPLEMENTATION

Our team is prepared for an aggressive but controlled 4-week rollout to ensure zero downtime for the brand.

Phase 1: Content Alignment (Week 1)

  • Data Porting: Migrating existing corporate history while integrating new, high-res photography.
  • Pricing Engine: Updating the backend with official, real-time pricing for all SKUs (Alcoholic, Soft Drinks, Water).
  • Geographic Mapping: Verifying and plotting updated distributor locations across the Northern, Central, and Southern regions.

Phase 2: System Integration (Week 2-3)

  • Lead Routing: Connecting all B2B order forms directly to Castel's internal sales emails or CRM.
  • Security Layer: Implementation of SSL encryption and secure tiered logins for verified distributors.
  • Testing: Rigorous mobile testing to ensure the site functions perfectly on entry-level smartphones.

Phase 3: Launch & Training (Week 4)

  • Live Deployment: Transitioning from the Vercel demo to the primary domain.
  • Internal Handover: A comprehensive training session for the Marketing and I.T. teams on updating news, prices, and distributor lists.

  • 5 Managed Updates: Five "Major Update" credits for new features or structural changes, valid for 3 years.

The Castel-Wrld project is not just a redesign; it is a declaration of market leadership. By digitizing the relationship between the brewery and the consumer, Castel Malawi will gain a significant competitive advantage in data collection and sales efficiency.
